The site you're reading this on went through about fourteen ideas before it finally stood still.

## A short, slightly embarrassing list

There was a fire one. A "your work floats in space" one. A version that was, briefly and inexplicably, a spreadsheet. Each felt clever for roughly a day, and then felt like a costume.

The idea that stuck is the most boring to describe out loud: a room you move sideways through, a few real projects, and an easy way to say hello. It turns out "clear" beats "clever" almost every time — it just takes a while to admit it.

## What I kept

- One strong idea, executed quietly, instead of five loud ones fighting for attention.
- Real work up front, instead of paragraphs about myself.
- A layout you understand in about three seconds.

If you're redesigning something and you're on version nine, quietly wondering whether you've lost the plot: you probably haven't. You're just getting close.
