One of my favourite projects last year was a website for a small bakery in Vilnius.

## Warm, not sweet

The brief was basically a feeling: "cozy, but not childish." No spec, no sitemap — just a shelf of genuinely beautiful cakes and a lot of trust.

So I did what you'd do walking into a good bakery. Let the product be the hero. Keep the type warm but confident. Add just enough motion that the page feels alive — and not one animation more.

## The lesson

Small businesses rarely need _more_ website. They need the _right_ one: fast, clear, and unmistakably theirs. The bakery didn't want a platform. It wanted to look as good online as its cakes did on the counter.

I think about that brief a lot. Most of my work is, one way or another, trying to bottle a feeling someone couldn't quite put into words.
